// CORE ARCHITECTURAL PHILOSOPHY

My approach is built on three foundational pillars. Each one ensures that the final output is not just a design, but a robust, manufacturable, and profitable asset.

Design for Manufacturing (DFM)

A perfect 3D model is useless if it can't be made. My foundation is in understanding the physical constraints of both additive (3D printing) and subtractive (CNC) processes. I design with tolerances, wall thicknesses, and tool clearances as primary parameters, not afterthoughts.

Generative Automation

Repetitive manual tasks are the enemy of efficiency. I leverage Python APIs for SolidWorks and Fusion 360 to build intelligent scripts that automate geometry generation, file preparation, and feature recognition. This turns hours of manual work into seconds of computation.

Business-Driven Engineering

Every technical decision must serve a business outcome: reducing costs, speeding up time-to-market, or eliminating errors. I quantify my work in ROI—saving hours of labor, reducing material waste, and preventing costly machine crashes.
